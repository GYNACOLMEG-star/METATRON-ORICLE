Padmasambhava

GURU RINPOCHE · THE WHITE LOTUS DEMON TAMER · 8TH CENTURY CE

Padmasambhava — "Born from the Lotus" — appeared in Tibet when Buddhism could not take root because every monastery was destroyed by demons, every teacher driven out. The King sent for him. He traveled alone into every mountain pass, every haunted valley, every dark corner where a demon lived.

He did not kill them. He appeared before each one in wrathful form — more terrifying than the terror itself — and made them an offer: "Serve the Dharma, or I will destroy you. Choose." Every demon chose to serve. He gave them each a job. He made them Dharma Protectors — guardians of the very teachings they once destroyed.

He is always depicted holding a lotus — born from mud, opening toward light. Untouched by the mud he rose from. The White Lotus.
